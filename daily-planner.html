Privacy by design

The model runs on your machine

Ollama downloads the AI model to your computer. When OatSmart generates assignments, it talks to that local model — not to a server in a data center.

Questions

Honest answers.

If you're wondering whether this is too technical, too AI-y, or too good to be true — read on.

Does it require internet to work?
After the initial Ollama download (which does require internet), the Daily Planner works fully offline. The AI model runs on your computer. No Wi-Fi, no hotspot, no cloud account required to generate assignments.
What grades does it support?
Pre-K, Kindergarten, 1st Grade, 2nd Grade, 3rd Grade, 4th Grade, and 5th Grade. Assignments are automatically calibrated to the grade level of the learner you select — the language, complexity, and expectations all shift accordingly.
Can I print the assignments?
Yes. Every generated assignment prints cleanly, formatted with your student's name, grade level, and the date at the top. You can print directly from the planner or save to PDF and print later.
Is this included in my plan?
The Daily Planner is included in the OatSmart Premium subscription at $6.99/month — no extra charge. If you're on the free trial, you'll have 14 days to try it before your subscription begins. Cancel anytime.
Is "Ollama" safe? What is it?
Ollama is a free, widely used, open-source tool for running AI models locally. It's used by developers and researchers around the world. It doesn't send any data online once the model is downloaded — it runs entirely on your computer. OatSmart's built-in setup guide walks you through installing it step by step.
How is it different from ChatGPT?
ChatGPT and similar tools are cloud services — everything you send them is processed on a remote server. The Daily Planner runs a local AI model on your own computer. Nothing you type, no information about your child, and no generated content ever leaves your device. It's also free to run — no ChatGPT subscription required on top of OatSmart.
